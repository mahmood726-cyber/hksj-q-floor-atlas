# E156-PROTOCOL — hksj-q-floor-atlas v0.1.0

**Project name:** hksj-q-floor-atlas
**Dates:** 2026-05-12 (spec) → 2026-05-12 (v0.1.0)
**Dashboard:** https://mahmood726-cyber.github.io/hksj-q-floor-atlas/
**Primary estimand:** median CI-width ratio (floored ÷ un-floored) across D₁ᵃ.

---

S1: How large is precision overstatement when the `max(1, Q/(k−1))` HKSJ floor is omitted under I²=0, across the full Cochrane CDSR?
S2: cochrane-modern-re's SHA-pinned REML outputs covered all 6,386 Pairwise70 MAs; Pairwise70 study-level yi/vi provided arm data.
S3: Every MA with I²=0 and k≥2 (D₁=3,677; 57.6%) had un-floored HKSJ CIs recomputed via independent metafor; CI-width ratio and significance-loss were derived.
S4: On the well-defined D₁ᵃ subset (Q>0, n=3,536) the median CI-width ratio was 3.64× (IQR 1.60–10.56; 95th pct 57.4×); significance was lost in 17.08% of MAs.
S5: Ratio ≥ 1 and no sig-gain held for all D₁ᵃ MAs; D₁ᵇ (Q=0, n=141) lost significance in 24.8% with unbounded ratio.
S6: The floor applies to a majority of the corpus, triples CI widths at the median, and is mandated by RevMan-2025; omitting it is not a minor oversight.
S7: v0.1 establishes prevalence under re-pooling only; matched-to-published CI audit and PM/SJ sensitivity are deferred to v0.2.
